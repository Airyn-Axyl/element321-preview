Section 05

Your Design Journey

Every project follows a thoughtful, proven process. This ensures nothing falls through the cracks and you always know exactly where things stand.

1

Discovery

Weeks 1–2

We begin with you. Through conversations, a style questionnaire, and a visit to your space, we learn how you live, what you love, and what your home needs to feel like yours. This is where we really get to know each other and set the tone for the entire project.

2

Concept Development

Weeks 2–4

This is where the magic starts. We develop mood boards, color palettes, and a design direction that captures your vision. We present, discuss, and refine until it feels just right. Every space has a melody, and this is where we find yours.

3

Design Presentation

Weeks 4–6

Room by room, we present detailed plans: furniture layouts, finish selections, fabric choices, lighting, and hardware. Everything is curated to work together beautifully. You’ll see the complete picture before a single order is placed.

4

Refinement & Approval

Weeks 6–8

Your feedback shapes the final design. We refine selections, adjust details, and finalize everything. For finishes, you’ll come in and walk through tile, wood, wallpaper, paint, and hardware samples together. The tactile experience matters.

5

Procurement & Project Management

Weeks 8–20+

Behind the scenes, we order, track, inspect, and coordinate every item. Lead times for custom furnishings and materials can range from 4 to 16 weeks. We manage the logistics so you don’t have to, and you’ll receive regular updates through your portal.

6

Installation & Styling

1–3 Days

This is the day it all comes together. Our team installs, places, and styles every piece. From furniture arrangement to the final throw pillow, we handle every detail so you walk into a finished space.

7

The Reveal

The Best Day

Welcome to your new favorite place. We walk you through every room, celebrate the transformation, and make sure you love every inch of it. This is the moment that makes everything worth it.

What Can Affect Timelines

A few things can shift your project schedule, and all of them are normal:

  • Decision timing — timely feedback keeps everything on track
  • Lead times — custom and imported items can take 8–16 weeks
  • Construction schedules — for new builds, design follows the build pace
  • Seasonal availability — vendor demand can occasionally affect timelines

We’ll always communicate proactively about timeline shifts. Your portal will show real-time project status so you’re never guessing.

Section 08

Your Role in the Process

You’re the most important part of this process. Here is how we make the most of our time together.

Share Openly

The more we know about how you live, what you love, and what drives you crazy, the better we can design for you. There are no wrong answers. Tell us about the morning light you love, the drawer that always jams, the color you can’t stand, the heirloom that stays no matter what.

Decide with Confidence

When we present options, we guide you through the reasoning. We ask for feedback within 5–7 business days to keep your project on schedule. Timely decisions are the single biggest factor in maintaining timelines.

Trust the Journey

Great design unfolds in stages. There may be moments when individual pieces don’t yet make sense on their own. That’s normal. The full picture comes together beautifully. We’ve done this before, and we’ll guide you through every step.

Stay Engaged

Attending meetings, responding to communications, and completing action items on time keeps everything moving. We schedule check-ins at natural milestones so you’re always in the loop without feeling overwhelmed.

Working With Your Builder

If you’re doing new construction or a renovation, we work hand-in-hand with your builder. We attend site meetings, coordinate directly on timelines, and make sure design decisions align with construction progress. Your builder will also have portal access for their assigned projects.

A note on changes: We include reasonable revisions in every project. If you change your mind about a selection before it is ordered, no problem. If a major design direction change is needed after approvals, we’ll discuss scope and any additional investment transparently before proceeding.

Section 12

Questions & Answers

What if I don’t like a design direction?

We include revision rounds in every project phase. If something doesn’t feel right, tell us early. We’d much rather adjust course than have you live with something you don’t love. Honest feedback is the best feedback.

Can I source my own items?

We’re happy to incorporate pieces you’ve found or already own. Just let us know early so we can integrate them into the design plan. We’ll give you our honest assessment of how they’ll work in the overall scheme.

What happens if something is back-ordered or delayed?

Supply chain realities mean occasional delays are normal. When this happens, we proactively source alternatives, adjust the installation schedule, or find creative interim solutions. We’ll always keep you informed.

Do you work with our contractor or architect?

Absolutely. We collaborate closely with builders, architects, and other vendors. For new construction and renovations, this coordination is essential. We attend site meetings, share specifications, and keep everyone aligned.

What if our budget changes mid-project?

Life happens. If your budget shifts, let us know as soon as possible. We’ll reprioritize selections and adjust the plan to stay within your new parameters without compromising the overall design vision.

What happens after installation?

We don’t disappear after reveal day. We’ll follow up to make sure everything is perfect and address any items that need attention. Many clients continue working with us on additional spaces over time.

How do I prepare for our first meeting?

Complete the Inspiration Homework section of this guide, think about how you use your spaces daily, and bring any Pinterest boards or saved images you love. The more we know about your taste and lifestyle, the better we can design for you.

What makes Element321 different?

You work directly with Heather and Rachel from start to finish. No hand-offs, no junior designers making decisions on your behalf. Every project gets our full attention, and we genuinely care about creating something you’ll love for years.

How Billing Works

We structure payments in phases: an initial design retainer to begin, a midpoint payment at selection approval, and a final payment before installation. Furnishing and material costs are billed separately as items are ordered.

Design fees are separate from product costs. Industry guidance suggests budgeting design services at roughly 10% of your overall project investment, though this varies by scope.

No surprises. You’ll always know what to expect before we move forward. We discuss investment openly and honestly because we believe trust is the foundation of great design.

Section 14

Inspiration Homework

Before we dive into design, we’d love to see what speaks to you. This helps us understand your taste faster and ensures we’re aligned from the very start. Don’t overthink it. If you love it, save it.

  • › Collect 10+ inspiration photos. From Houzz, Pinterest, Instagram, magazines, or anywhere you find beautiful spaces. Save kitchens, bathrooms, living rooms, textures, colors, moods. The more variety, the better we can find patterns in what you’re drawn to.
  • › Create or share a Pinterest board. If you already have one, send us the link. If not, start a board called “Element321 Inspo” and pin anything that catches your eye. We’ll review it together.
  • › Follow @element321design on Instagram. See our latest projects and behind-the-scenes process. We share a lot of design thinking in our Stories.
  • › Follow Element321 on Pinterest. Browse our curated boards for style inspiration. Feel free to save anything that resonates to your own boards.
  • › Note what you want to feel in each room. Cozy? Bright? Calm? Energized? Moody? The emotional quality matters as much as the visual style. Write a few words for each room we’ll be designing.
  • › Think about how you use each space. Where does the family hang out most? Where do you work? Where do the kids do homework? Where do guests gravitate? These patterns shape design decisions.
  • › Identify must-haves and dealbreakers. Family heirloom that stays? Color you absolutely can’t stand? Material you’ve always dreamed of? Tell us everything.
  • › Take photos of your current space. Even if it’s messy. Snap wide shots of each room, plus close-ups of anything specific. Upload them to your portal.

Where to submit: Upload inspiration photos and space photos to the Photos & Inspiration section of your client portal. Or create a shared Pinterest board and send us the link at hello@element321design.com.
