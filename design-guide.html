Section 07

What to Expect

Clear expectations are the foundation of a great working relationship. Here’s what you can count on from us, and what helps us do our best work for you.

Communication

Our hours: Monday–Friday, 9am–5pm CT

Best way to reach us: Email or your client portal

Response time: Within one business day, often sooner

Updates: Regular progress updates via portal, plus check-in calls at key milestones

Budget

We design to your budget, not beyond it. Transparency about your investment range helps us serve you well. Design fees are separate from product costs. Changes after approval may affect scope; we’ll always discuss before proceeding.

Decisions

At key milestones, we present curated options and ask for your approval. We recommend 5–7 business days to review. You’ll get our best recommendation, a strong alternate, and the confidence to choose. Reasonable revisions are included in every phase.

Collaboration

We work directly with your architect, builder, and vendors as a team — coordinating on specifications and timelines. Keep us in the loop on independent design decisions so we can maintain a unified vision.

Section 08

Your Role in the Process

You’re the most important part of this process. Here is how we make the most of our time together.

Share Openly

Tell us how you live, what you love, and what drives you crazy. The morning light you love, the drawer that always jams, the color you can’t stand, the heirloom that stays no matter what. There are no wrong answers — only useful ones.

Decide with Confidence

When we present options, we walk you through the reasoning. Timely decisions — within 5–7 business days — are the single biggest factor in maintaining your timeline.

Trust the Process

Great design unfolds in stages. Individual pieces may not make sense on their own before they come together. We’ve done this before, and we’ll guide you through every step.

Working With Your Builder

For new construction and renovations, we work hand-in-hand with your builder: site meetings, direct coordination on timelines, and making sure design decisions stay aligned with construction progress.

A note on changes: Reasonable revisions are included in every project. If you change your mind before an item is ordered, no problem. Major direction changes after approvals are discussed openly before any additional investment is made.

Section 12

Questions & Answers

What if I don’t like a design direction?

We include revision rounds in every phase. If something doesn’t feel right, tell us early. Honest feedback is always the best feedback.

Can I source my own items?

Yes — let us know early so we can integrate them into the plan. We’ll give you our honest assessment of how they’ll work in the overall scheme.

What happens if something is back-ordered or delayed?

Occasional delays are normal. We proactively source alternatives, adjust schedules, and always keep you informed.

Do you work with our contractor or architect?

Absolutely. For new construction and renovations, this coordination is essential. We attend site meetings, share specifications, and keep everyone aligned.

What if our budget changes mid-project?

Let us know as soon as possible. We’ll reprioritize and adjust the plan to stay within your new parameters without compromising the overall vision.

What happens after installation?

We follow up to make sure everything is perfect and address any items that need attention. Many clients continue working with us on additional spaces over time.

How do I prepare for our first meeting?

Complete the Inspiration Homework in Section 14, think about how you use your spaces daily, and bring any saved images or Pinterest boards. The more we know upfront, the better we can design for you.

What makes Element321 different?

You work directly with Heather and Rachel from start to finish. No hand-offs, no junior designers making decisions on your behalf. Every project gets our full attention.

How Billing Works

Payments are structured in phases: a design retainer to begin, a midpoint payment at selection approval, and a final payment before installation. Furnishings and materials are billed separately as items are ordered. Industry guidance suggests budgeting design services at roughly 10% of your overall project investment, though this varies by scope.

No surprises. We discuss investment openly and honestly because trust is the foundation of great design.

Section 14

Inspiration Homework

Before we dive in, we’d love to see what speaks to you. This helps us understand your taste quickly and ensures we’re aligned from the start. Don’t overthink it — if you love it, save it.

  • › Collect 10+ inspiration photos. Houzz, Pinterest, Instagram, magazines — kitchens, baths, textures, colors, moods. Variety is good. We find patterns in what you’re drawn to.
  • › Create or share a Pinterest board. If you have one, send us the link. If not, start one called “Element321 Inspo” and pin anything that catches your eye.
  • › Follow @element321design on Instagram. See our latest projects and design thinking. We share a lot in our Stories.
  • › Note how you want each room to feel. Cozy? Bright? Calm? Moody? The emotional quality matters as much as the visual style.
  • › Think about how you use each space. Where does the family hang out? Where do the kids do homework? These patterns shape design decisions.
  • › Identify must-haves and dealbreakers. Heirloom that stays? Color you can’t stand? Material you’ve always dreamed of? Tell us everything.
  • › Take photos of your current space. Wide shots of each room plus close-ups of anything specific. Upload them to your portal.

Where to submit: Upload to the Photos & Inspiration section of your client portal, or send a Pinterest board link to hello@element321design.com.
